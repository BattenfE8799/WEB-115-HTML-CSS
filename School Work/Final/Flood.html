Flood:

A flood is an overflow of water that submurges land that is usallly dry. This can be a few inches or even several feet high. In 1993, the Mississippi River flooded at 49.58 feet high. The 1844 Great Flood was even bigger than that.

Preparing for a Flood

Before:

Have a plan.

What todo:

Have emergency supplies above first floor, and find out how to turn off the electric, gas, and water mains.

Be Ready:

Watch the weather. Get weather alerts and notifications.

What to do:

  • Get to a safety.
  • Leaving when advised is the best plan.
  • However, if you can't, turn off your electicity, gas, and water mains.
  • Have extra water and other supplies.
